1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They help sites remember your preferences and provide a smoother experience. SmashOne uses cookies only where necessary for the service to function and to measure aggregate site performance.

This page describes cookies used on smashone.us and app.smashone.us.

2. Essential cookies

These cookies are required for the service to work. You cannot opt out, though you can disable cookies entirely in your browser (the service may not function).

3. Analytics cookies

We use Plausible Analytics, a privacy-friendly analytics tool. Plausible does not use cookies for cross-site tracking, does not store personal data, and is GDPR/CCPA compliant by design. Plausible counts page views and unique visitors using a daily-rotating salted hash of IP+User-Agent — no individual user tracking.

If you prefer to opt out of analytics, see section 5.

4. Third-party cookies

SmashOne marketing pages (smashone.us) do not embed third-party trackers. The dashboard (app.smashone.us) may load resources from:

  • Stripe — payment forms set Stripe.com cookies on their hosted checkout. See Stripe's cookies policy.
  • OAuth providers (Meta, Google, Telegram) — when you authorize SmashOne to access your social accounts, those providers' OAuth flows may set their own cookies during the redirect handshake.

5. Managing cookies

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Common controls:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data

Disabling essential cookies will prevent the dashboard from functioning. Analytics are already privacy-friendly (no opt-in required), but if you wish to block them, browser-level Do Not Track or a content blocker will prevent the Plausible script from loading.

6. Changes

We will update this page if our cookie practices change. Material changes will be notified by email to active customers.
